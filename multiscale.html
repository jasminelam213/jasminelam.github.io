Multi-Scale Recipe

In COGS 125, I decided to create a multi-scale design prototype on Figma for a recipe app.

Although there are recipe websites, their format is not user friendly.

Roles

User Interface (UI) Designer

Project Specifications

Duration: 2 Days

Tools

  • Figma

Overview

In this project, I designed a multi-scale interactive prototype in Figma to address usability issues commonly found in recipe websitesâ€”particularly the need for constant scrolling, which disrupts the cooking experience. The goal was to reimagine recipe interaction across three physical display scales: inch-scale, foot-scale, and yard-scale, each tailored to different user contexts.

Problem

  • Most recipe websites are not optimized for real-world cooking scenarios. Users are forced to scroll constantly, navigate through ads, pop-ups, and long blog intros just to access step-by-step instructions or ingredient lists. This creates a frustrating and inefficient experience, especially when hands are messy or time is limited in the kitchen.

Problem Solution

  • Inch-scale for quick-glance access (e.g., timers or steps on a smartwatch)
  • Foot-scale for interactive browsing (e.g., managing ingredients or editing servings on a tablet)
  • Yard-scale for hands-free, large-display use (e.g., step-by-step guidance on a smart kitchen screen)
  • This solution reduces cognitive load, minimizes the need to touch or scroll, and improves usability by tailoring the interface to the physical context in which cooking happens.

The Process

When I first started this project, I had a minimal understanding about what it meant to create an inch-scale, foot-scale, and yard-scale design. I had to do some research on what it meant to create a multi-scale design. I learned that it is important to consider the physical context in which the user will be using the product. I also learned that it is important to consider the user's needs and how they will interact with the product at different scales. For my first design I had replicated the messages app on the Apple Watch.

Outcome

I realized that the inch-scale design was not very useful for a recipe app. I then decided to create a new inch-scale design that would be more useful for a recipe app. I created a new inch-scale design that would allow the user to quickly glance at the ingredients and steps of the recipe without having to scroll. I also created a foot-scale design that would allow the user to interact with the recipe in a more hands-on way, such as e diting the servings or managing the ingredients. Finally, I created a yard-scale design that would allow the user to follow the recipe step-by-step without having to touch or scroll.

Inch-Scale Design

Task: Sarah needs to make dinner because she has not eaten all day. Her hands tend to get dirty and wants to quickly glace at the instructions on the spot. Sarah does not know how to cook and wants to follow a recipe.

Foot-Scale Design

Task: Task: Sarah and John both just got off of work and came home hungry because they have not eaten all day. They want to quickly glance at the ingredients and steps. They want to focus on different steps and ingredients so they can quickly finish making dinner. They both do not know how to quick and want to follow a recipe.

Yard-Scale Design

Task: Sarah and her friends needs to make dinner together because they have not eaten all day. Their hands tend to get dirty and they want to quickly glace at the instructions on the spot. Sarah and her friends do not know how to cook and wants to follow a recipe. They tend to talk together in a group setting and get distracted.
